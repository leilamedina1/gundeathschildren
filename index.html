What is the month with more child gun-related deaths?

Children and minors are particularly vulnerable to gun violence. Recent years have seen an increase in recorded deaths among children and there is a month where most deaths occur.

By Leila Medina

November 29, 2023

- New York, New York - Gun-related issues persist as a recurring topic in the United States. Among the various demographics affected by this issue, children and minors stand out as particularly vulnerable. In 2023, there have been 1555 recorded deaths among children according to Gun Violence Archive â€”a number that has seen an alarming increase in recent years, a trend exacerbated by the ongoing pandemic. The American Academy of Pediatrics highlights this surge, "In 2020, pediatric firearm deaths increased 28.8%, a surge compared with uptrends in years before the coronavirus disease 2019 pandemic."

Historically July has witnessed a higher incidence of child deaths related to guns, even during periods when children were at home. A critical aspect to consider is that, despite the absence of physical attendance at schools during certain periods, the accessibility of firearms at home remains a prevalent threat. The Population Reference Bureau highlights a statistic, revealing that "For the 32% of children whose fatal injury was self-inflicted, easy access to guns made that possible."
